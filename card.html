African Bush Elephant

African elephants are the largest and heaviest land animal on earth.

  • Scientific Name: Loxodonta africana
  • Distribution: Sub-Saharan AFrica
  • Max. Height: 3.96 meters
  • Max. Weight (male): 10.4 tonnes

African bush elephants have very large ears and a trunk. The female elephants are very social animals. They live in herds of related females and their offspring. The adult males usually live alone and only approaching the females of a herd during the mating season. The matriarch leads the herd from water source to water source. There's a very strong bond between the individual herd members. If a females gives birth, the other females acknowledge the new member by touching it with their trunks. If a member of the herd dies, the herd stays with the corps for a while.
